# Product Vision

## Product definition

ForjaDev Marketplace is a premium marketplace for digital game-server resources, starting with Rust and designed to expand into more games and creator ecosystems over time.

It is not only a catalog of downloadable files. It is a complete operating system for:

- buyers who need to discover, trust, purchase, access and maintain resources with confidence
- sellers who need to publish, update, support and commercialize products professionally
- admins who need to moderate, govern and audit the platform with low operational friction

## Current product direction

The marketplace should feel stronger and more coherent than a basic plugin store. Every major surface should reinforce one of these capabilities:

- discovery
- trust
- secure commerce
- post-sale continuity
- seller operations
- governance and safety

## North star

ForjaDev should become the most reliable place to buy and operate server resources:

- buyers can move from discovery to post-sale resolution without friction
- sellers can maintain a live product from one coherent workspace
- admins can enforce quality, security and policy with strong internal tooling

## Core user journeys

### Buyer
- discover a product or bundle
- evaluate quality, trust and seller credibility
- complete checkout cleanly
- access the purchase from orders, licenses and downloads
- re-download safely when needed
- move into support or dispute flows with context preserved

### Seller
- create and edit products
- publish releases with a clear lifecycle
- support customers by product
- manage campaigns and coupons by product
- understand the operational state of a live product from one workspace

### Admin
- review and moderate products
- manage licenses, disputes and support oversight
- govern public editorial trust surfaces
- audit critical actions and react to risk signals

## Product pillars

### 1. Discovery
- clear public catalog structure
- category, game and product surfaces with real intent
- architecture ready for stronger SEO and ranking

### 2. Trust
- visible policies and support expectations
- seller reputation and operational signals
- help center and public marketplace rules

### 3. Secure commerce
- clean checkout architecture
- protected downloads
- licenses as an operational access layer
- clear relationship between order, license and download rights

### 4. Post-sale continuity
- buyer orders as post-purchase hub
- redownload and access-state clarity
- support and dispute continuity without fragmentation

### 5. Seller operations
- product workspace as the seller command center
- explicit release lifecycle
- support and commercial tooling by product

### 6. Governance and safety
- moderation workflows
- audit logging
- risk visibility
- editorial guardrails

## Non-negotiable product standards

- Public pages must support real user intent and should not exist as thin placeholders.
- Buyer-facing access states must be explicit, not ambiguous.
- Seller-facing tooling must feel operational, not like disconnected CRUDs.
- Admin surfaces must optimize for control, traceability and safety.
- Every important flow must respect permissions, data integrity and route stability.
- Every major capability should be verifiable through reproducible QA whenever practical.

## Current strategic focus

The project is currently strengthening the marketplace as a coherent system, not chasing shallow feature breadth.

This means prioritizing:

- solid buyer journey and post-sale operations
- strong seller product operations
- trust and policy infrastructure
- permissions, QA and safe lifecycle rules

## Explicit non-goals for now

These areas may come later, but should not displace current platform-hardening priorities unless explicitly reprioritized:

- broad SEO expansion without architectural readiness
- multilingual rollout
- public discovery expansion that ignores trust or operational quality
- shallow analytics features without clear product leverage

## Success criteria

ForjaDev is succeeding when:

- a buyer can purchase and regain access without confusion
- a seller can run a live product from one coherent workspace
- public trust surfaces reduce uncertainty before and after purchase
- admins can manage safety and policy without hidden operational debt
